# SEEL — Use Case

---

Sarah is a software engineer in Austin. She earns $3,200 a month, has been at the same company for four years. Consistent income, never missed a payment.

But in DeFi, none of that matters.

She deposits $5,000 worth of ETH into a Solana lending protocol. The protocol tells her: you can borrow up to $3,250. 65% LTV. Because to the protocol, she is just a wallet address. Her salary, her track record, her reliability — all invisible.

---

**SEEL bridges this gap.**

Sarah connects to Plaid through SEEL. Her bank statements open in the browser — they never leave her device, never touch a server. A ZK circuit running locally answers a single question:

*"Has the monthly average income exceeded $2,000 for the past 6 months?"*

Answer: yes. A proof is generated.

The proof contains no name. No employer. No exact figures. Just a cryptographic statement: *this wallet's owner has sufficient income.*

She pays a $3 attestation fee. A Soulbound token is minted on Solana — 30-day validity, non-transferable, stamped Tier 1.

The lending protocol reads the token and raises her LTV limit to **80%**.

Same $5,000 ETH. Sarah can now borrow $4,000 — without revealing her identity, without sharing her data, simply by proving the truth.

---

> **SEEL:** Prove your real-world income without revealing it. Borrow more on Solana.
